# Evidence Quality Rules

These rules apply whenever research data is being evaluated for confidence, scoring, or inclusion in a result, across any pipeline in this plugin.

- If stored research data uses vague or unquantified language (examples: "sometimes," "generally friendly," "fairly common," "usually good"), do not treat this as strong evidence.
- Lower the confidence level assigned to that claim, and state plainly that the underlying language was vague.
- Prefer specific, dated, sourced claims over general reputation or unattributed statements.
- If a claim has no source or date, treat it as weaker evidence than one that has both, even if the content sounds similar.
- Never present a vague claim as if it were a confident, quantified fact.
